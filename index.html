Smart Assist Headset

I designed "Smart Assist™" a wearable headset for visually impaired people developed to describe the person's surroundings, read things such as menus and gauge facial expressions using holophonic sound, 3D scanning and maping sensors. I focused on making information available to visually impaired people easily without depending on others. The design won the Social Impact Award and was one of Dr. Stearn's favorite projects. Later, I filled a patent in India and licensed its rights with HCL technologies. I plan to work on this project this summer and want to focus on the value sensitive design of the model and its design refinement.

User interaction Intern

I got the opportunity to be an UX designer Intern where my clients were TATA ClassEdge. Our team was focused on the availability of the interface and CMF design components, to design a smart board that can be used by teachers and students in rural areas of India. During my internship I learned a lot of new skills like Control drawing and designing the experience architecture for the smart board interface. Our goal was to make the interface as simple and engaging as possible, make it easy for them to access the information and upload data even with low bandwidth internet. I got the opportunity to meet and work with some amazing people from around the world! More than anything, I am proud to be a part of this project and empower the next generation of India with better education.

Physics research - international journal

Vacancy mediated spin polarization and magnetism in Graphene monolayer A Full potential approach. Graphene was discovered in 2004 as the simplest two-dimensional material based on C-atom and has a variety of unusual physical properties, which are essential for future nano-electromechanical systems (NEMS). Manipulating the novel properties of graphene, such as carrier concentration or magnetism by utilizing defects is an area of research with important ramification in the deployment of graphene for devices. The purpose of our study is to take a closer look on the nanostructure of Graphene to generate new spintronic devices with a greater power and efficiency.
